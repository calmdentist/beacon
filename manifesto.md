# Beacon

## A New Social Primitive for the LLM Era

### Turn live inquiry into connection

---

## Abstract

Large language models have changed how people think.

Millions of users now use AI not just to generate text, but to explore questions, test ideas, synthesize research, and work through uncertainty. For the first time, a mainstream technology can understand what a person is actively trying to figure out.

And yet almost all of this intelligence remains trapped in private, single-player sessions.

This is the missing primitive.

**Beacon is a new social layer for the LLM era.** It lets a user turn a valuable exploration inside their LLM into a structured object — a **Beacon** — and discover who else is thinking about the same thing, or who can meaningfully advance the inquiry.

If search helped us find information, and social networks helped us find audiences, Beacon helps us find the **right minds at the right moment**.

---

## The Shift

The internet was built around:

* pages
* feeds
* profiles
* followers
* search queries

The AI era introduces a new object:

## **live inquiry**

Not who you are in general.
Not what you publicly post.
Not what keywords you search.

But:

* what you are exploring right now
* what you believe
* where you are stuck
* what kind of help would unlock you

This is new.

Before LLMs, software could not deeply understand a person’s active curiosity. Now it can.

But the default mode is still isolated:

* one user
* one model
* one private thread
* one private result

That is useful, but incomplete.

Some of the most valuable outcomes in human progress come from the right collision:

* the one person asking the same strange question
* the researcher with the missing method
* the skeptic who saves months of wasted effort
* the collaborator you did not know existed

LLMs can now see the contours of these collisions before humans can.

Beacon exists to make them happen.

---

## The Problem

Today, when a user has an unusually good conversation with an LLM, there is no native next step.

A thought may be:

* too niche for social media
* too early for public posting
* too valuable to lose in a chat history
* too dependent on reaching the right person, not the largest audience

Existing products do not solve this.

### Search finds documents.

It does not find the right living mind.

### Social media finds audiences.

It does not reliably find the right collaborator, expert, or co-explorer.

### Professional networks find profiles.

They do not capture what someone is actively thinking about this week.

### LLMs understand the user.

But they do not yet route that understanding socially.

That is the gap.

---

## The Insight

The next major social primitive will not be built on identity first.

It will be built on:

## **live intent**

A Beacon is the simplest expression of that primitive.

A Beacon is a structured representation of a live inquiry:

* what I’m exploring
* what I know
* what I need
* who could help

It is not a transcript.
It is not a post.
It is not a static profile.

It is a socially actionable unit of thought.

---

## The Product

A user is deep in conversation with their LLM of choice.

At some point, the interaction stops feeling like a chat and starts feeling like a real inquiry:

* a research direction
* a startup idea
* a scientific question
* a difficult concept worth pressure-testing
* a rabbit hole that deserves other minds

At that moment, the user invokes a simple action:

## `@Beacon this`

Beacon turns the inquiry into a clean, structured object.

Then Beacon can do two things:

### 1. Preserve the inquiry

A user can save and revisit the idea as something more useful than chat history.

### 2. Route the inquiry

Beacon can show:

* who else is thinking about this
* who is approaching it from an adjacent angle
* who has the expertise the user is missing

This is the core behavior.

Not posting.
Not broadcasting.
Not performing.

Routing.

## Design Principles

### 1. Live inquiry over static identity

What matters most is not who you said you were years ago, but what you are trying to understand now.

### 2. Precision over reach

Beacon is not about broadcasting to the most people. It is about routing to the right people.

### 3. Opt-in by default

Not every thought should become social. Beacon only exists when the user chooses.

### 4. Fewer, better collisions

The goal is not engagement for its own sake. The goal is a small number of highly valuable connections.

### 5. Human connection, AI mediation

AI should help package, understand, and route inquiry. The real value comes from the resulting human exchange.
